## Section Task

Build the conceptual vocabulary needed for the rest of the review. Compare games as materials, programs, systems, research objects, social infrastructure, and spatial practices. Preserve distinctions among full games, gamification, and play, and explain why they imply different mechanisms and evaluation strategies.

Compare analog, digital, live, immersive, and hybrid forms by affordances, barriers, social configuration, visibility of rules, automation, platform dependence, accessibility, and lifecycle cost. Do not construct an analog-versus-digital hierarchy.

Translate game-studies debates into library decisions: competition and collaboration, rules and narrative, abstract mechanics and cultural situatedness, intrinsic activity and extrinsic reward. Use adjacent theory cautiously.

Treat playful architecture and environment as part of GBLS while addressing noise, privacy, safety, spectatorship, age zoning, and coexistence with quiet uses. End by explaining why library purposes, rather than modalities, should organize the next section.
