## Integration Pass

Read the assembled manuscript as a whole. Revise boundaries between sections so concepts develop cumulatively rather than restarting. Harmonize terminology, citation style, evidentiary language, and authorial voice. Remove unnecessary repetition while preserving each section's substantive coverage and approximate word target.

Check that every H1 and H2 begins with a synthesis claim rather than an
individual study. Ensure the service-purposes and programming sections do
different intellectual work: service purposes must explain why libraries use
games, while programming models must explain how particular formats enact,
constrain, or complicate those purposes. Add an explicit bridge between these
sections and test that no format is treated as having a single inherent
purpose.

Preserve explicit outliers and null findings. Consult the section ledgers and
corpus coverage audit to correct material coverage imbalances. Do not
introduce evidence absent from the coded summaries.

Consult the baseline prose audit during integration. Baseline prose may be
condensed, reorganized, or qualified, but every substantive idea must remain
traceable or have an explicit omission reason. Check that applicable claims
have evidence and that unsupported authorial propositions are labeled as such.

Run a bidirectional citation check on the integrated manuscript. Carry every
unresolved record into the references verification subsection and reviewer
notes rather than supplying guessed metadata.

Save the integrated manuscript as `2-outputs/SECOND_DRAFT.md`.
