### Summary
# **gnomedic. (2024). *LSHM: TTRPG Summer Break*. Google Docs.**

This short Low Stress High Mayhem handout supports a streamed session of gnomedic's *Summer Break*. It identifies the game, links to the Twitch VOD, points to gnomedic's social, Twitch, itch.io, and portfolio pages, and notes that the game appears in *The Ultimate Micro-RPG Book*, edited by James D'Amato. The document then gathers play-alikes, safety tools, GameRT links, Twitch chat norms, and a practical stream outline.

The play-alike section is organized around mood and audience rather than formal genre alone. It groups recommendations under "summer and nostalgia vibes," "kid at heart vibes," and "fort vibes," including games such as *Sleepaway*, *Camp Flying Moose for Girls of All Kinds*, *Two Summers*, *An Altogether Different River*, *Crescent Moon*, *BFF! Best Friends Forever*, *The Tragedies of Middle School*, *Kids on Bikes*, and *Fort*. This makes the handout a useful example of player advisory language for tabletop games: the recommendations connect theme, tone, life stage, and social feeling.

The document also embeds facilitation norms. It points to *Script Change*, *The Door Is Open*, and the TTRPG Safety Toolkit, then gives a Twitch policy emphasizing kindness, respect, no hate speech, moderator authority, harassment reporting, and private contact. The stream summary moves from tech check to introduction and rules, character creation, fort creation, danger creation, actual play, emotional check-in, and a post-play discussion about rules, playtesting, playing at different life stages, what childhood means to different people, programming potential, safety-tool considerations, and chat questions.

For the larger GBLS project, the item is useful as a practitioner artifact showing how an online game program can be scaffolded around theme, advisory context, safety, and reflection. Its strongest GBLS relevance is practical: it demonstrates how librarians or library-adjacent facilitators might frame a small TTRPG not just as entertainment, but as a structured event with discovery paths, moderation policy, emotional check-ins, and discussion prompts about memory, childhood, and life-stage interpretation.

This source should nevertheless remain outside the main literature review. It is not scholarship, not an evaluation, and not a sustained case study of library service. It may be useful later for an appendix on game advisory, stream facilitation, TTRPG safety resources, or low-stress online programming, but it should not be cited as evidence for GBLS outcomes.
