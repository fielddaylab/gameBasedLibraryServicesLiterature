### Summary
# ***LSHM: TTRPG Venture Society*. (2024). Google Docs.**

This short Low Stress High Mayhem resource document is an advisory and session-support handout for *Venture Society*, a tabletop role-playing game associated here with 9th Level Games. The document opens with a link to the Twitch VOD for the session and then gathers search terms, play-alikes, online articles, video recommendations, safety resources, scholarly readings, a Twitch chat policy, GameRT links, and a brief stream outline.

The terms-to-explore section frames the game around tabletop role-playing games, cozy games, roleplaying-focused games, nonviolent TTRPGs, and kids' TTRPGs. The online articles point toward introducing children to RPGs and role-playing with younger players, while the video recommendation highlights wholesome or low-pressure roleplaying. The play-alike section groups recommendations by audience and mood: games for kids and tweens, games with Redwall-like cozy animal fantasy, and games with broader fantastical-world vibes. In this sense, the document functions like a player-advisory record rather than a research article.

The document is also notable for the way it combines game recommendations with facilitation and safety infrastructure. It points viewers to the TTRPG Safety Toolkit and includes a Twitch policy that emphasizes kindness, respect, no hate speech, moderator intervention, harassment reporting, and private contact. The included scholarly citations cover social and emotional learning, interpersonal communication, autistic tabletop roleplayers, emotional resiliency, transformative role-playing, LGBTQIA+ group therapy, and educational motivation. These citations show the surrounding discourse that GameRT staff or volunteers considered relevant when contextualizing a low-stress TTRPG stream.

For the larger GBLS project, the item is useful as a practitioner trace of how a library-adjacent game community packaged a streamed tabletop session. It shows a service pattern that combines actual play, reader/player advisory, age-appropriate recommendations, safety norms, related scholarship, and community links. That pattern may be valuable for designing future program guides, collection records, or facilitator notes for cozy and youth-friendly TTRPG programming.

Even so, this source should remain outside the main literature review. It is a primary resource handout, not scholarship, not an evaluation, and not a sustained description of a library service. It can inform practical appendix work on advisory language, safety-resource bundling, and stream facilitation, but it should not be cited as evidence for GBLS outcomes, patron learning, or implementation effectiveness.
