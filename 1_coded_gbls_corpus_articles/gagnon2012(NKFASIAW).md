## Purpose & Scope

This 2012 blog post by game developer Simon Ludgate makes a provocative argument: the solution to stagnation in the video game industry is to hire librarians. Writing in response to industry commentary about large game publishers releasing formulaic "clones" rather than innovative titles, Ludgate argues that libraries and librarians represent untapped expertise and creative thinking that the game industry needs.

Rather than directly addressing games-based library services, this piece approaches the topic from the inverse angle: what libraries and librarians offer to *other* sectors. It reflects early-2010s discussions about librarian expertise and professional identity.

## Key Arguments

- **Creative Problem-Solving:** Ludgate, drawing on conversations with a medical librarian, argues that librarians are exceptionally creative and unconventional thinkers despite working in seemingly traditional institutions
- **Information Expertise:** Librarians are information professionals skilled in organizing, maintaining, and facilitating use of information repositories and knowledge systems
- **Organizational Innovation:** Librarians bring expertise in project management, digital records management, and system design that could benefit creative industries
- **Contrast with Game Industry:** The piece argues that game studios, despite being creative institutions, often exhibit "hostile opposition to change, crushing oppression of creativity, and refusals to consider unconventional solutions"
- **Professional Qualifications:** Suggests game companies should include MLIS degree requirement in hiring to gain access to librarian expertise

- Does not cite research or data supporting claims about librarian capabilities
- Addresses game industry needs, not library adoption of games
- Very brief treatment; lacks substantive development of core arguments
- Published on a game industry blog (Gamasutra), not a library or academic venue
