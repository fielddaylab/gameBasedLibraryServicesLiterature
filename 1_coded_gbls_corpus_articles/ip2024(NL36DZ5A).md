## Purpose & Scope

This 2024 case study documents the establishment, development, and circulation of a video game collection at the University of Alberta Library, established in response to new academic programming in game development. The article provides practical documentation of collection development strategy, vendor relationships, circulation patterns, and challenges encountered over approximately six years (end of 2017 through 2023).

The study offers concrete evidence of how academic libraries can build and maintain game collections while addressing practical operational challenges—particularly pre-publication (pre-pub) ordering arrangements with game vendors and the resulting inventory management issues.

## Key Case Details

### Collection Development:
- **Initial Batch:** 56 games purchased December 2017; circulation began Q1 2018
- **Growth Trajectory:** Expanded to 122 games across 8 platforms by end of 2023 (total 125 acquired; 3 missing)
- **Annual Budget:** Modest CAD ~$1,000/year
- **Platforms:** Supports multiple console platforms and PC games

### Strategic Approach:
- **Faculty Collaboration:** Initial selection guided by Computer Game Development Certificate program faculty
- **Pre-Publication Partnerships:** Established standing orders with game vendors for specific publishers, developers, and game series
- **Developers/Publishers Targeted:**
  - Developers: BioWare, Telltale Games
  - Publishers: Bethesda, Rockstar Games, Ubisoft
  - Series: Assassin's Creed, BioShock, Dragon Age, Grand Theft Auto, Mass Effect, Elder Scrolls, Tomb Raider
- **Local Interest:** Considerations included local game industry connections (e.g., BioWare founded in Edmonton by UofA alumni)

### Circulation Patterns:
- Games circulate with varying frequency by platform and title popularity
- Most circulated titles include Minecraft, Assassin's Creed series, Final Fantasy series
- Pre-pub arrangements ensure collection stays relevant to course syllabi and student interests

## Implementation Conditions & Strategies

- **Curricular Alignment:** Collection tied to specific academic program needs
- **Vendor Partnerships:** Pre-publication arrangements critical for maintaining relevant, current collection
- **Geographic Relevance:** Local industry connections (BioWare in Edmonton) influenced selection
- **Modest Budget:** Demonstrates collection development possible with limited financial resources
- **Cross-Platform Support:** Facilities and circulation systems must accommodate multiple gaming platforms

## Operational Challenges Documented

- **Pre-Publication Ordering Issues:**
  - Complexity of standing orders across multiple publishers/developers/series
  - Potential for unnecessary acquisitions if game release plans change
  - Managing inventory when vendor relationships/agreement terms shift
  
- **Platform Diversity:**
  - Different platforms (PS4, Xbox, PC, Switch, etc.) require different physical storage and circulation considerations
  - Potential wear/damage to discs and cases with high circulation
  
- **Relevance Management:**
  - Games quickly become outdated; "trending" games must be constantly refreshed
  - Balance between curricular needs and broader student interests
  - Limited shelf life compared to print collections

## Service Outcomes

- **Course Support:** Collection provides materials directly supporting game development coursework
- **Student Access:** Eliminates barriers for students unable to afford game purchases
- **Recreation/Engagement:** Games available for personal use, increasing library patronage
- **Program Visibility:** Game collection signals institutional support for game-related programs

## Identified Gaps & Limitations

- **No Outcome Evaluation:** Does not assess whether collection supports course learning or student success
- **Circulation Only:** Limited data on non-circulation engagement (browsing, discovery, educational impact)
- **No Comparative Analysis:** Does not compare circulation patterns across institutions or analyze demographics of users
- **No Cost-Benefit Analysis:** Does not evaluate cost-effectiveness of collection or budget allocation priorities
- **Digital Distribution Challenge:** Article notes shift toward digital distribution; unclear how UofA addressing access to digital-only releases
- **Vendor Dependency:** Collection development heavily dependent on continued vendor relationships and pre-pub availability
- **Sustainability Questions:** Unclear whether collection is sustainable long-term given modest budget and platform diversity

## Broader Implications

- Game collections achievable with modest budgets through strategic vendor partnerships
- Curricular alignment critical for justifying collection development and ensuring relevance
- Pre-publication arrangements important for keeping current collection relevant to academic programs
- Physical game collections face headwinds as industry shifts to digital distribution
- Libraries need new models for acquiring digital-only games (not addressed in this case)
