## Purpose & Scope

This 2025 article in *Pennsylvania Libraries: Research & Practice* provides librarians unfamiliar with tabletop roleplaying games, particularly Dungeons & Dragons 5e (the most current edition), with practical guidance for implementing TTRPG programming in their libraries. Published in an era when TTRPG popularity has surged (supported by shows like *Stranger Things* and *Critical Role*), the article addresses the resource needs and implementation questions facing librarians considering game programs.

The article draws on research about TTRPG benefits, documents current practice in libraries, and provides both resource recommendations and guidance on getting started without prior gaming experience.

## Key Findings & Arguments

- **Documented Benefits of TTRPGs:**
  - Social skill development (teamwork, leadership, working with diversity)
  - Creative and collaborative problem-solving
  - Critical thinking through scenario navigation
  - Confidence-building and relationship formation
  - Improved literacy and comprehension through rulebook engagement
  - Math skill development through dice calculation
  - Spatial reasoning through map/grid work
  - Neurodiverse accessibility and communication support

- **Learning & Literacy Benefits:**
  - Reluctant readers develop comprehension skills through rulebooks
  - Fantasy and lore exploration encourages recreational reading
  - Information literacy applications (e.g., bias exploration through character roleplay)
  - Multi-modal learning engagement

- **Library & Community Benefits:**
  - Serves as intergenerational programming
  - Acts as community hub and "third place"
  - Attracts new/returning patrons
  - Provides social-emotional learning difficult to deliver through traditional programming
  - Supports mental health and resilience through escapism and socialization

## GBLS-Specific Contributions

- **Pandemic Response:** Documents how libraries rapidly pivoted TTRPG programming online during COVID-19, sustaining engagement and community during isolation
- **Post-Pandemic Sustainability:** Notes continued and growing demand for TTRPG programming post-pandemic
- **Multiple Audience Segments:** Demonstrates appeal across teens, young adults, and adults; serves diverse populations including neurodiverse participants
- **Social-Emotional Learning:** Explicitly frames TTRPGs as vehicles for teaching empathy, teamwork, decision-making, and conflict resolution
- **Practical Implementation:** Provides specific resource lists, platform recommendations (Roll20, D&D Beyond, Discord), and guidance for new facilitators

## Implementation Framework

- **Facilitator Training:** Libraries need staff trained in game mechanics and facilitation techniques
- **Campaign vs. One-Shots:** Offers both sustained engagement (campaigns) and accessibility (one-shot adventures)
- **Collection Support:** Recommends specific books and materials to support programs
- **Platform Flexibility:** Documents both in-person and online delivery models with specific tools
- **Accessibility Considerations:** Emphasizes inclusivity and accommodation of diverse players

## Service Conditions Emphasized

- Skilled facilitation is critical; librarians may need training or recruitment of experienced facilitators
- Inclusive design necessary to serve neurodiverse and marginalized participants
- Infrastructure needs: physical space for in-person or technology for online delivery
- Sustainable staffing models to support ongoing programs

## Identified Gaps & Limitations

- Limited empirical outcome data; primarily synthesizes existing research and practitioner experience
- Does not address budget constraints or resource scarcity in underfunded libraries
- Limited discussion of barriers (administrative resistance, staff expertise gaps, facility constraints)
- Focuses specifically on D&D 5e; limited discussion of other TTRPG systems
- No comparative analysis of different implementation models (in-person vs. online, librarian-led vs. teen-led, etc.)
- Sustainability and long-term impact not addressed
