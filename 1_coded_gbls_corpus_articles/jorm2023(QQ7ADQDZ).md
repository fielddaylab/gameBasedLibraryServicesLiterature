## Purpose & Scope

This 2023 practitioner article by Mali Jorm, a library innovator focused on information management in school settings, explores educational immersive experiences—particularly escape rooms and breakout boxes—as library programming. Grounded in learning science and flow theory, the article makes the case for immersive experiences as pedagogically powerful and sustainable library services.

Rather than empirical research, this is reflective practice-based guidance drawing on Jorm's experience designing immersive educational experiences in school libraries. It synthesizes theory (Csikszentmihalyi's flow, Kidd and Nieto McAvoy's immersive experience framework) with practical implementation wisdom.

## Key Concepts & Arguments

### Flow and Immersion:
- **Flow State:** Csikszentmihalyi's concept of deep engagement characterized by challenging goals and presence
- **Immersive Experience Goals:** Recreate flow states in educational contexts where students become "highly engaged and able to forget the world around them"
- **Optimal Experience:** Immersive experiences aim to create "occasions where we feel exhilaration, a deep sense of enjoyment"

### Four Aspects of Successful Immersive Experiences:
(Drawing on Kidd & Nieto McAvoy, 2020)

1. **Social Experience:** Shared experiences improve belonging, reduce isolation; shared debriefing consolidates learning
2. **Emotional Engagement:** Emotional investment increases empathy and desire to continue learning about topics
3. **Embodied & Spatial Interaction:** Students move and interact physically/sensiorially; create new neural connections; experience agency and control
4. **Authenticity:** Meaningful, genuine connections to content and purposes

### The Multisensorial Dimension:
- Immersive experiences offer contrast to "bounded learning" (page/screen-limited)
- Activate multiple senses: movement in space, touching objects, verbal interaction
- Provide "true 3D experience" compared to screen-limited engagement
- Create sensorial reality grounding in physical and emotional dimensions

## School Library & Teacher Librarian Role

Jorm emphasizes that:
- **School Libraries as Immersive Space:** Libraries are natural locations for creating immersive experiences; unique architectural and programmatic resources
- **Teacher-Librarian as Designer:** Teacher librarians bring design thinking, pedagogy, and creative skills to immersive experience creation
- **Complex Undertaking:** Creating immersive experiences requires significant design time investment but yields:
  - High student engagement
  - Robust learning outcomes
  - Enduring resources reusable with different student cohorts

## Design Framework for Immersive Experiences

Jorm provides implicit framework emphasizing:
- **Emotional Connection:** Design for emotional resonance, not just intellectual challenge
- **Authentic Challenge:** Puzzles and scenarios have genuine problem-solving significance
- **Social Scaffolding:** Build in collaborative elements and community sharing
- **Embodied Engagement:** Incorporate movement, tactile interaction, spatial navigation
- **Shared Reflection:** Debriefing after experience consolidates and extends learning

## Benefits for Learners

- Contrast to typical educational environments (sitting, screens, passive reception)
- Agency: students move, speak, interact at own pace
- Creativity: open-ended exploration within structured framework
- Confidence-building: safe environment to try new approaches
- 3D spatial and multisensorial learning compared to page/screen

## Implementation Considerations

- **Time Investment:** Designing immersive experiences requires substantial preparation
- **Reusability:** Well-designed experiences can be reused with multiple cohorts, amortizing design investment
- **Sustainability:** Library systems can create enduring resources rather than one-time programs
- **Facilitation:** Requires skilled teacher-librarians comfortable with design and open-ended learning
- **Diverse Learners:** Flexibility in rules and pacing enables differentiation for diverse abilities

## Identified Limitations & Gaps

- **Primarily Conceptual:** No empirical data on outcomes of immersive library experiences
- **No Assessment Framework:** Limited guidance on how to evaluate learning outcomes from immersive experiences
- **Implementation Barriers Not Addressed:** Does not discuss resource constraints, time limitations, or administrative barriers in under-resourced schools
- **Equity Considerations:** Limited discussion of how to make immersive experiences accessible to all students (cost, physical accessibility, neurodiversity)
- **Evidence Claims:** While grounded in flow theory, specific effectiveness claims not empirically validated
- **Generalizability:** Draws on school library context; applicability to other library types unclear
