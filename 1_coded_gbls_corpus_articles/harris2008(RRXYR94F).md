## Purpose & Scope

This edited collection represents one of the earliest comprehensive treatments of video game collections and programs in academic libraries. Published by the Association of College and Research Libraries (ACRL) in 2008, it serves as both a practical guide and a case study compendium showcasing pioneering academic libraries implementing game collections and game-based instruction.

The volume brings together chapters from librarians and faculty at multiple institutions describing their experiences establishing video game collections, developing game-based curricula, and using games for information literacy instruction. It covers collection development, marketing strategies, curricular integration, and pedagogical applications.

## Key Findings & Contributions

- **Collections Development:** Documents early practices for building video game collections in academic libraries, including selection criteria, circulation models, and vendor relationships
- **Curricular Integration:** Demonstrates how libraries can position games as pedagogical resources supporting academic programs, particularly in computer science and game development
- **Information Literacy:** Shows multiple models for integrating games into information literacy instruction, using game mechanics and game-based scenarios for teaching research and critical thinking
- **Faculty Partnership:** Emphasizes collaborative development between librarians and faculty to identify relevant games and meaningful integration points
- **Marketing & Discovery:** Addresses how academic libraries can promote game collections to students unfamiliar with the concept of library-provided games

## GBLS-Specific Contributions

- **Pioneering Case Studies:** One of the earliest documented case studies of GBLS in academic library settings (2008)
- **Service Ecology:** Demonstrates interconnections between collections development, programming, instruction, and faculty partnerships
- **Audience Engagement:** Shows how games increased student engagement with library spaces and services
- **Learning & Literacy Outcomes:** Documents use of games for information literacy and orientation instruction
- **Institutional Capacity:** Addresses staff training and professional development needed to support game-based services

## Implementation Conditions Identified

- Successful programs required faculty collaboration and curricular support
- Staff needed training on game content, platforms, and pedagogical applications
- Adequate budget and vendor relationships essential for building relevant collections
- Clear communication/marketing needed to overcome assumptions about games in academic settings

## Identified Gaps & Limitations

- No rigorous outcome evaluation; primarily descriptive case studies
- Limited evidence of sustained programs or long-term impact
- Focus on physical media; digital distribution challenges not addressed (relevant now in 2025)
- Limited discussion of equity and access barriers
- Primarily serves academic library context; less applicable to other library types
