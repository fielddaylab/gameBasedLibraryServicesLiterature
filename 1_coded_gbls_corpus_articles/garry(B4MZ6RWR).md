## Purpose & Scope

This Scottish Book Trust resource is a practitioner-focused guide designed to help teachers and librarians understand tabletop roleplaying games (TTRPGs), particularly Dungeons & Dragons, and their educational value. Rather than presenting empirical research, it synthesizes expert knowledge from educators and librarians who have implemented TTRPG programming in school and library settings.

The guide provides practical information about what TTRPGs are, how they work, their alignment with Curriculum for Excellence (Scotland's educational framework), how to get started running games, and examples of games suitable for use with children. It is explicitly designed for audiences new to tabletop RPGs.

## Key Findings & Arguments

- **Learning Mechanisms:** TTRPGs develop multiple literacies and skills through:
  - Social interaction and collaborative problem-solving
  - Creative thinking and improvisation
  - Oral communication and listening
  - Reading comprehension through rulebook engagement
  - Critical thinking through scenario navigation

- **Curriculum Alignment:** TTRPGs support key learning outcomes in Curriculum for Excellence including literacy, numeracy, health and wellbeing, and skills development

- **Accessibility & Inclusivity:** Minimal equipment required (paper, pencils, dice); games can be adapted for different ability levels and learning needs

- **Psychological Benefits:** Games provide safe environments for exploring new identities, practicing social skills, and building confidence in reluctant learners

## Implementation Guidance

- **Essential Materials:** Paper, pencils, dice (minimal cost barriers)
- **Facilitation Role:** Game Master or facilitator guides narrative and adjudicates rules; does not require extensive prior experience
- **Game Selection:** Multiple TTRPG options available beyond D&D; can choose based on learning goals and group interests
- **Classroom Integration:** Can be used as standalone activity or embedded within curriculum units
- **Ongoing Engagement:** Campaigns develop over multiple sessions; promotes sustained engagement and relationship-building

## Service Conditions Emphasized

- Skilled facilitation essential; facilitators need training on game mechanics and educational goals
- Safe, collaborative environment critical for full benefit realization
- Differentiation and accessibility accommodations necessary for inclusive participation
- Flexibility in rules and pacing supports diverse learners

## Identified Gaps & Limitations

- Primarily focused on UK context (Curriculum for Excellence); transferability to other educational systems unclear
- Does not address school or librarian barriers (time, training, administrative support)
- Limited evidence on long-term impact or sustainability of programs
